Coastal Eutrophication

Coastal eutrophication is the accumulation of excessive nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus in coastal waters, often from sources such as agricultural runoff and sewage discharge. This leads to algae overgrowth, blocking sunlight and depleting oxygen levels, which harms marine life and biodiversity. Mitigating coastal eutrophication requires reducing nutrient inputs through improved agricultural practices and wastewater treatment.

Ocean
Acidification

Ocean acidification, caused by rising atmospheric carbon dioxide levels, lowers the pH of the oceans, harming marine life such as corals and shellfish. This disrupts ecosystems, impacting fisheries and livelihoods. Mitigation involves reducing CO2 emissions and enhancing ecosystem resilience.

Ocean
Warming

Ocean warming, driven by climate change, disrupts marine ecosystems, affecting habitats, species, and biodiversity. It worsens coral bleaching, sea level rise, and impacts weather patterns, coastal communities, and ocean-dependent economies. Addressing it requires cutting greenhouse gas emissions and implementing adaptation measures.

Plastic
Pollution

Plastic pollution is the build-up of plastic waste in the environment, especially oceans and rivers. It harms marine life through ingestion and entanglement, threatens ecosystems, and can affect human health through the food chain. Solutions involve reducing plastic use, improving waste management, and promoting sustainable alternatives.

Over
-fishing

Over-fishing happens when fishing rates surpass the natural replenishment of fish stocks. It depletes populations, disrupts ecosystems, and threatens livelihoods. Solutions involve implementing effective fisheries management measures to ensure sustainability.
